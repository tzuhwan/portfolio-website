Data Visualization with D3.js

In this project I used d3.js, a powerful interactive visualization library, to create an interactive dashboard that explores a Netflix dataset that consists of tv shows and movies available on Netflix as of 2019.

Some questions that I focused on are:

  • What are the top genres in terms of number of title on Netflix?
    • I grouped the number of movies and TV shows by genre, and then filter for titles that only have one single genre, such as "Documentaries", as opposed to "International Movies, Sci-Fi" to provide clear overviews on basic genres without sub-genres. This also helped to keep the chart visually neat.
  • How does the average runtime of movies evolve over the years?
    • Given the huge range of years included in the data set, I calculated the average movie lengths for every 5 years from 1945 to 2020. I also provided two sorting orders that users can toggle to observe different patterns.
  • What do the networks of actors/actresses who collaborated on movies look like?
    • I narrowed down the data that I needed to display by selecting only the actors who acted in Dramas Films in 2018, as the cast data for all year range is too massive. I picked the Dramas genre because it is more likely that actors in the same genres collaborate with one another. So each node is an actor/actress, and the movie in which they collaborate are the links between them. Each cluster more or less represent a movie.
